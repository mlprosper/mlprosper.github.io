--- layout: post title: Facebook Questions and why it sucks tags: - User Interfaces status: publish type: post published: true meta: _edit_last: '19924537' jabber_published: '1304487160' comments: true --- Recently I had a question to ask of parents of school-age children.  After talking with a couple of friends, we decided the best course of action would be to use the build-in Facebook Questions feature.  While it worked, there were some BIG gotchas that make the feature less then stellar.  I created a question with 5 options: As you can see, the display of the question is far from optimal.   First of all, why is the question truncated?  There are only 3 more words after the ellipse - why cut them off when there seems to be plenty of room to place them on the same line?  Ok, fine, I get it that FB wants to keep their questions short.  That I can live with. What I can't live with is the inexplicable decision to truncate each option when there is plenty of room on the right to show the whole text.  The first option only has 4 more characters for goodness sake!  As it is, only 3 of the 5 options are displayed.  It gets worse from there... Clicking on the "2 more" link opens this window: If I click on "2 more", doesn't that mean I would like to see 2 more options?  Why continue to obscure the last 2 choices when I just clicked a link that says "2 more"? Even though the window is wider now, the text of the choices is still truncated!  I can't even choose to expand the options - I have to hover my mouse over them to see the full text in a tooltip.  Madness!  As if other Facebook users don't have anything else to do but hunt and peck to read the options of my stupid little poll. It gets even better if I try to edit: If I click on "Edit Options", doesn't that imply that I can, hmm.. say, EDIT the options?  Well, Facebook has decided that I can't EDIT the options, only delete them.  They also decided that I can't edit the question itself from this window.  I guess I have to delete and recreate the poll to do that. Facebook, you get a big fat "F" for your Question tool. It's a good start, but if I can point out these basic flaws in my first run through of the feature, they should have been caught in any kind of quality assurance process.   You can do better.
