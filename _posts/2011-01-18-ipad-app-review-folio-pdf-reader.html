16:57:35";} comments: true --- I've tried a lot of PDF readers for the iPad, but Folio has the best combination of features and simple design to keep me coming back to it every time I get a new book. Book Cataloging Starting the app brings you to this really elegant Cover Flow style book listing: My catalog of books is getting fairly long, so swiping all the way to the left and right takes a bit of time.  It's a little annoying, but fortunately there's no lag when swiping.  Additionally, tapping the upper right icon the brings up an alphabetical listing of books: As far as I know, there's no way to create "categories" or "folders" of books like in iBooks or GoodReader.   You're stuck with the nice but inefficient coverflow and the alphabetical listing. Importing Files From within the Folio app, PDFs can be imported directly from web sites, iDisk locations and Dropbox - that's it.  My primary method of importing files has been to email it to myself and use the "Open In.." button in Mail to transfer the file to Folio.  Those looking for integration with a larger variety of 3rd party services should go with a different app. Reading Folio reads like a traditional book.  You "turn" pages by swiping or tapping left and right.  It's similar to the iBooks experience, without the fancy page turn animation.  Tapping on the right moves the view to the bottom of the page or to the next page.  Tapping on the left moves to the top of the current page or the previous page.  Tapping the center of the screen brings up the menu.  The biggest difference from iBooks is that Folio expands the current page to the full width of the iPad by default. However, if I zoom in on a page, then turn the page, the view snaps back to the full page width.  The zoom level is not preserved.  I've never found this to be an issue when reading in landscape on the iPad, but for some people, this missing feature may be a dealbreaker. Searching and Bookmarks Folio does a superb job at detecting the existing PDF markers and displaying them in an accessible menu.  Bookmark titles are also editable allowing me to create meaningful bookmarks instead of a simple page number or snippet of text from the page. Summary Pros: Elegant, clean design Great bookmark detection and editing Simple, effective reading gestures Fast Cons: Limited Import options No custom categories/folders for books No zoom preservation when turning pages Overall Rating: 4 out of 5
