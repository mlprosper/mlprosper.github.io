'{{unknown}}' comments: true --- Recently my friend Paul asked me for a ride from the San Jose Airport. I was running a little late and needed to let him know how late I would be, so I decided to use a web app at http://nrout.com. I entered San Jose Airport as my destination and copied the resulting link to a text: When Paul opened the link on his smartphone, he could see a map of my location in real-time: I set this up before I started driving and didn't have to do anything else during the trip. No texting to update him, no calls, and no wondering where the other person was. The only caveat was that I needed to keep my phone from going to sleep so that the GPS signal would keep updating. That only required an occasional tap on the screen. Once I got closer to the airport the map automatically zoomed in. I thought Paul was at Terminal A but could quickly see that he wasn't there since i was still 0.2 miles from him while driving past the terminal. This was a super easy way to make airport pickups a little easier.  As a pickup-er, I could let Paul know where I was in real-time.  As a  pickup-ee, Paul knew whether to drag his family out to the curb or just comfortably wait inside a little longer. UPDATE: NROUT is no longer in active development.
